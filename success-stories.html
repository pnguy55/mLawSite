$2 MILLION
WRONGFUL DEATH

When a family lost their mother, hit by a speeding police car, our team fought for compensation from both the city police department and nearby gas station, whose signs had blocked the view of the road. $1.95 MILLION
BOATING ACCIDENT

The clients were involved in a boating accident. Plaintiff underwent a cervical fusion, two shoulder surgeries, and head injury. The defense argued the plaintiff experienced post concussion syndrome that should have resolved within 6 months. The passengers case settled for $1.95 million and the operator of the boat was paid $275,000. The passengers case was settled 5 days before trial. The plaintiff had no lost wage claim.

$1.8 MILLION
WRONGFUL DEATH

A Murphy Law Firm client received a $1,800,000 jury verdict. A 34-year-old male grabbed an electric line 12 inches from a rooftop and was electrocuted. The father made a claim for the death of his son. The electric line violated the National Electric Safety Code (NESC) and the protective insulation was worn off. Entergy of Louisiana claimed that the deceased son had 50% fault even though Entergy is considered a superior actor. After a three day trial, the jury awarded $1,800,000. The jury held Entergy 65% at fault and the son 35% at fault for his death.

$750 THOUSAND
BICYCLE ACCIDENT

The Plaintiff was injured when he was traveling through a parking lot near LSU. After going over a speed bump, the front tire of the bicycle hit a pot hole causing the plaintiff to flip over the handlebars. The Plaintiff injured his arm and was required to undergo surgery. $750 THOUSAND
18-WHEELER ACCIDENT

An 18 wheeler side swiped the Plaintiffs vehicle while changing lanes of travel. The truck driver denied liability and attempted to blame the plaintiff. During the deposition of defendant driver, it was proven that the plaintiff controlled the lane of travel. The biomedical engineer and physician(s) connected the injury and resulting need for back surgery due to this side swipe. The client underwent a lumbar fusion after consecutive treatment. The defendant paid $750,000.00 resolve this claim.
